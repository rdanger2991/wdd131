Port-au-Prince, Haiti

Official Flag of Haiti

Haiti is a Caribbean country that shares the island of Hispaniola with the Dominican Republic. It is the second-oldest republic in the Western Hemisphere and the first independent nation in Latin America. Haiti covers 27,750 square kilometers (10,714 sq mi) and is home to approximately 11 million people.

Haiti has a rich cultural heritage, being the first Black-led republic in the world when it gained independence from France in 1804. The country is known for its vibrant art, music (particularly Kompa), literature, and religious traditions including Vodou. Haiti's landscape features beautiful mountain ranges, with the highest peak being Pic la Selle at 2,680 meters (8,793 ft), as well as coastal plains and valleys.
